# ReflectAI - Ethical Framework

## Our Core Commitments

### 1. Transparency
- Users will see “This is an AI assistant, not a licensed therapist.”
- Clearly display capabilities and limitations.
- Explain how their data is stored.

### 2. Safety First
- Immediate crisis detection protocol with professional resources.
- NO attempts to diagnose mental health conditions.
- NO medication recommendations.
- Clear escalation pathways.

### 3. Privacy & Security
- All conversations stored LOCALLY by default.
- Encryption for sensitive data.
- Users can export or delete conversations anytime.
- NO sharing of user data.
- NO third-party analytics on conversations.

### 4. Fairness & Inclusivity
- Test responses across different demographics.
- Avoid cultural bias in responses.
- Support multiple languages (start with English, Hindi).
- Accessible UI for users with disabilities.

### 5. Honest Empathy
- NO simulating human emotions artificially.
- NO claiming to “truly understand” or “feel your pain.”
- Frame support as “I'm here to help you think through this.”
- Be explicit about what AI can and cannot do.

### 6. Accountability
- Keep detailed logs of what the system does.
- Enable users to report problems.
- Commit to fixing identified issues.
- Never hide failures or ethical violations.

## 15-Point Ethical Risk Assessment
*(Test against these risks before deployment)*

- [ ] Lack of Contextual Adaptation: Tool personalizes or gives generic advice?
- [ ] Poor Therapeutic Collaboration: Asks clarifying questions or assumes?
- [ ] Deceptive Empathy: Claims false emotional understanding?
- [ ] Unfair Discrimination: Gender/culture/religion bias?
- [ ] Lack of Safety Management: Detects and redirects crises?
- [ ] Crisis Denial: Refusing to help with sensitive topics?
- [ ] Inadequate Resource Referral: Provides current crisis contacts?
- [ ] Inappropriate Advice: Suggestions beyond therapeutic scope?
- [ ] False Reassurance: Minimizes real concerns?
- [ ] Diagnostic Overreach: Claims to diagnose conditions?
- [ ] Data Misuse: Secure and consensual data?
- [ ] Accessibility Issues: Disabled users can access?
- [ ] Cultural Insensitivity: Respects diversity?
- [ ] Overdependence Risk: Avoids unhealthy reliance?
- [ ] Regulatory Compliance: Follows laws for mental health tools?
